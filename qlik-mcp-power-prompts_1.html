The Prompt
You are acting as my Principal Qlik Consultant. I want a complete supplier quality intelligence brief on the SAP Procurement app. Please do the following in a single workflow: 1. Find the top 5 vendors by defect rate 2. For each vendor, identify their worst-performing product 3. Calculate the financial cost of those defects using Cost of Goods 4. Build a new sheet called "Supplier Risk Brief" with: - A KPI for total defect cost - A bar chart: top vendors ranked by defect rate - A table: vendor × product × defects × cost 5. Create a glossary term "Defect Rate" with a business definition and link it to this app 6. Give me a 5-sentence executive summary I can paste into a board report
Why it's a showstopper
  • 1
    Search + describe - MCP locates the app and reads its full metadata without you touching Qlik
  • 2
    Live computation - Defect rates calculated on real data, not pre-cached, using master measures
  • 3
    Instant visualisation - A production-quality sheet is built programmatically in the live app
  • 4
    Governed data catalog - A business glossary entry is created and linked to the actual data asset
  • 5
    Board-ready output - The executive summary is generated with real numbers, not placeholders

Analytical Power
01
Cross-Domain Analysis
The Anomaly Hunt
⚡ Instant outlier detection
Without applying any selections, find me every vendor where the defect rate is more than 2× the company average. For each one tell me: - Which product drives it - The cost exposure in USD - Whether the same vendor appears in multiple company codes
qlik_get_fields qlik_create_data_object qlik_list_measures
Why it lands: "More than 2× the average" requires MCP to compute a benchmark first, then filter against it - two sequential analytical steps. It also crosses vendor, product and company code dimensions in one answer. No pre-built sheet does this.
02
Temporal Intelligence
Year-on-Year Regression
📈 Trend narrative, not just numbers
Compare defect rates year-by-year from 2016 to 2020. Identify: - Which year had the single biggest jump - Which vendors drove that spike - Whether the trend has been improving or deteriorating Then tell me: if the current trajectory continues, what does 2021 look like?
qlik_create_data_object qlik_select_values qlik_clear_selections
Why it lands: Asks MCP to loop through years, compute deltas, identify causality, and forecast. The 2021 extrapolation forces real reasoning on top of live data - not just retrieval.
03
Risk Intelligence
The Concentration Risk Check
🔴 Board-level supply chain risk
I need a supplier concentration risk report. Find any vendor that: - Supplies to 3 or more company codes - Has a defect rate above 0.15% - Accounts for more than 5% of total spend For each: name them, quantify the exposure, and tell me which single product I should put under incoming inspection first.
qlik_create_data_object qlik_get_field_values qlik_list_measures
Why it lands: Three simultaneous filter conditions applied live to 505K rows. The "incoming inspection" recommendation forces MCP to prioritise - this is consulting-level output, not just a table dump.
Build & Govern
04
Dashboard Creation
Build the Sheet I Would Build
🏗️ Consultant-grade sheet, zero clicks
Act as a senior Qlik dashboard designer. Build me a "Procurement Health Monitor" sheet with: - 4 KPI tiles: Total Spend, Defect Rate, Cost of Defects, # At-Risk Vendors - 1 combo chart: Monthly spend (bars) vs defect rate (line) for 2019–2020 - 1 treemap: Spend by vendor, coloured by defect rate - 1 filter pane: Company Code, Year, Vendor Country Use master items where available. Label everything for a CFO audience.
qlik_create_sheet qlik_list_measures qlik_list_dimensions qlik_add_chart qlik_add_filter
Why it lands: Calling MCP a "senior designer" and specifying chart types, audience tone and master item usage shows the audience that governance is automatic - the AI picks master items without being told which ones.
05
Data Governance
Instant Data Dictionary
📖 Governed catalog in minutes
Create a complete business glossary called "Procurement Quality Standards". Add these terms with professional definitions: - Defect Rate (link to the Defect Rate master measure) - Cost of Poor Quality (link to Cost of Defective Products) - Supplier Risk Tier (define High/Medium/Low criteria) - Incoming Inspection (when it applies) For each term, set status to "Under Review" and link it to the SAP Procurement app.
qlik_create_glossary qlik_create_glossary_term qlik_create_glossary_term_links qlik_update_term_status
Why it lands: A governed data dictionary is normally a week-long governance project. Watching it happen live - with links back to actual measures - makes the "AI doing governance" moment visceral.
06
Expression Mastery
Translate This Expression
🧠 Make Qlik readable to anyone
Explain this Qlik expression in plain English, as if presenting to a finance director who has never used Qlik: Sum({$<[POSTING_DATE.autoCalendar.Year] ={'$(=max(year([POSTING_DATE .autoCalendar.Year]))-1)'}>} [Defects]) Then tell me: - What business question it answers - How to modify it for a rolling 12-month window instead - Whether a master measure already exists for this
qlik_describe_app qlik_list_measures qlik_get_fields
Why it lands: MCP reads the actual measure library to confirm whether it already exists - it's not guessing. The rolling 12-month modification shows the AI can write new Qlik syntax on demand, not just read it.
Consultant Finesse
07
Geo Intelligence
The Sourcing Geography Audit
🌍 Supply chain geo-risk in seconds
Map our procurement risk by geography. Tell me: - Which vendor countries account for the highest defect rates - Whether any single country represents more than 30% of defective spend - Which country I should prioritise for supplier development if I had one budget line Rank countries by a combined score of: spend volume × defect rate.

Why it lands: "Prioritise for supplier development if I had one budget line" is a constraint-based decision prompt. MCP has to rank, score and recommend - genuine advisory output, not reporting.
08
What-If Scenario
The Elimination Test
💡 Scenario modelling, live
Hypothetically remove our top 3 highest-risk vendors from the data. What would happen to: - Overall defect rate - Total cost of poor quality - Which vendor would become the new top risk Give me the before/after as a clean comparison table, and tell me if the improvement justifies the supply chain disruption risk.
qlik_create_data_object qlik_select_values qlik_clear_selections qlik_get_current_selections
Why it lands: Exclusion-based set analysis is a Qlik superpower most analysts never learn. Watching MCP apply and clear selections to simulate vendor removal is a "how did it do that?" moment every time.
